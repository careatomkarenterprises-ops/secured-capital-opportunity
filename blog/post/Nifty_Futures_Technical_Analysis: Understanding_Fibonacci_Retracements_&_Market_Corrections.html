2. What Are Fibonacci Retracements?

Fibonacci retracements are based on ratios derived from the Fibonacci sequence: 23.6%, 38.2%, 50%, 61.8%, and 78.6%. After a significant price move, traders plot these percentages to identify potential reversal levels. For example, if a stock moves from ₹100 to ₹200, a 61.8% retracement would be at ₹161.8.

These levels work because many market participants watch them – they become self‑fulfilling. Institutional algorithms also monitor these zones, adding to their significance.

Why 61.8% is special

Known as the "golden ratio," it appears frequently in nature and often marks the line between a healthy pullback and a trend reversal.

3. What the Chart Is Indicating

The current market structure suggests Nifty Futures is approaching a critical decision zone – a confluence of the 61.8% Fibonacci retracement and a previous demand area. When markets approach such zones, three outcomes are possible:

  1. Support Holds: Buyers step in and market bounces.
  2. Sideways Consolidation: Market stabilises for a few days.
  3. Support Breakdown: A strong selling wave pushes the market lower.

At present, traders should focus on the reaction near 23,666. A sustained hold could attract dip buyers; a decisive break with volume may open the door to 23,016.

4. The Role of Volume

Volume measures the intensity of market participation. The current sell‑off is occurring on higher‑than‑average volumes, which can indicate:

  • Institutional activity: Large players booking profits or adjusting positions.
  • Panic selling: Retail investors exiting en masse, sometimes creating a temporary oversold condition.
  • Climax selling: High volume near support can mark the end of a decline.

Always analyse volume in context. A low‑volume break below support may be a false move; a high‑volume break is more credible.

5. Market Psychology & "Big Money"

Sharp moves are often engineered by sophisticated players to create liquidity. They push prices to levels where stop‑loss orders cluster, triggering them and allowing large players to enter or exit at favourable prices. Media and social networks can amplify fear, causing retail investors to sell at the worst moment.

Understanding this helps you detach emotionally. Instead of reacting, observe whether the move is a genuine trend change or just noise. Disciplined traders focus on technical levels, volume, and risk management – not headlines.

Investor alert: Do not let fear drive your decisions. Ask yourself: is this sell‑off fundamentally justified, or is it a technical overshoot?
