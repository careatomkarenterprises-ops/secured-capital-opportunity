Returns & Payments

Understanding your monthly returns and payment process

1

Is the 2% monthly return guaranteed?

Yes, the 2% monthly finance charge is contractually obligated in our notarized bilateral agreement under the Indian Contract Act, 1872. This is not a discretionary return but a fixed finance charge for the capital provided.

We have maintained 100% payout punctuality since 2018 with zero missed payments across 500+ partners.

The agreement clearly specifies:

  • 2% fixed monthly finance charges
  • Payment date: 5th of every month
  • Mode: NEFT/IMPS transfer
  • Late payment penalty: 1.5% per day (never invoked)
2

When will I receive my monthly returns?

Monthly finance charges are credited on the 5th of every month via NEFT/IMPS transfer to your registered bank account.

Payment Schedule:

  • Month 1: Returns credited on 5th of following month
  • Subsequent months: Regular 5th of month schedule
  • Weekends/Holidays: Payment made on previous working day
Reference: Clause 4.2 - Payment Schedule in Bilateral Agreement
3

Can I increase my investment later?

Yes, you can increase your investment at any time. The process is simple:

  1. Submit written request for additional investment
  2. Fresh PDCs issued for additional amount
  3. Supplementary agreement signed
  4. Returns calculated from date of additional investment

Important: Additional investments follow the same security protocols and are covered under separate PDCs.

Security & PDC

How your principal is protected through banking instruments

4

How is my principal secured?

Your principal is secured through multiple layers of protection:

1. Post-Dated Cheques (PDC):

  • We issue PDCs for your full principal amount
  • Cheques remain in your physical possession
  • Dated for principal repayment date (13th month)
  • Enforceable under Section 138 of Negotiable Instruments Act

2. Notarized Agreement:

  • Legally binding contract signed before notary
  • Clearly defines all terms and obligations
  • Provides civil remedy in case of disputes

Your PDCs are your direct banking security - they never leave your possession.

5

What happens if I need to withdraw my principal?

Principal withdrawal follows a structured process:

90-Day Written Notice:

  • Submit written withdrawal request
  • 90-day notice period begins
  • Monthly returns continue during notice period
  • Principal returned on agreed date

Early Withdrawal (Before 8 months):

  • Allowed only in exceptional circumstances
  • Returns recalculated at 1.5% for actual period
  • Excess returns adjusted against principal
Reference: Clause 6.3 - Withdrawal Process in Bilateral Agreement

Legal Framework

Understanding the legal structure of our partnerships

7

Is this regulated by SEBI or RBI?

No. Omkar Enterprises operates under a specific legal framework:

Legal Status:

  • Private Bilateral Agreement: Under Indian Contract Act, 1872
  • Not SEBI Regulated: Not a collective investment scheme
  • Not RBI Regulated: Not an NBFC accepting public deposits
  • Exempt from BUDS Act 2019: As per Section 2(4) for business transactions

Why This is Advantageous:

  • Direct contractual relationship
  • Clear civil remedies
  • No regulatory compliance delays
  • Flexible partnership terms
8

What happens in case of disputes?

Dispute resolution follows a structured escalation process:

Step 1: Written Notice (30 days)

  • Parties attempt amicable resolution
  • Direct discussions between partners

Step 2: Mediation (30 days)

  • Mutually agreed mediator
  • Non-binding recommendations

Step 3: Arbitration (Final & Binding)

  • Single arbitrator in Pune/Thane jurisdiction
  • Under Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996
  • Costs shared equally

Important: All disputes are civil in nature only. No criminal proceedings unless fraud established in civil court first.
