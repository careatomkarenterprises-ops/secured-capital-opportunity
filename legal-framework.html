LEGAL FRAMEWORK

Bilateral Partnership Agreement

Our structured partnership model combines legal protection with flexible terms

Nature of Agreement

Clause 1.1 - 1.5
REF: OE-BLA-2024

Bilateral Loan Agreement

All partnerships with Omkar Enterprises are structured as Private Bilateral Loan Agreements under the Indian Contract Act, 1872.

Not a Public Deposit

This arrangement is strictly a private agreement between Omkar Enterprises and the partner. It does not constitute a public deposit, collective investment scheme, or banking activity.

No SEBI/RBI Regulation

As a private bilateral agreement, this partnership is not regulated by SEBI (Securities and Exchange Board of India) or RBI (Reserve Bank of India).

Exempt from BUDS Act 2019

This arrangement is exempt from the Banning of Unregulated Deposit Schemes Act, 2019 as it qualifies as an inter-corporate deposit between business entities.

SECURITY MECHANISMS

Multi-Layer Protection

Comprehensive security measures ensure principal protection and enforceability

Security Mechanisms

Clause 2.1 - 2.8
REF: OE-SEC-2024

Post-Dated Cheques (PDC)

Issued for the full principal amount at agreement signing. These cheques remain with the partner as primary security.

Enforceable under: Section 138 of Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881

Notarized Agreement

Legally binding contract signed in presence of a notary public. Contains all terms, conditions, and obligations of both parties.

Personal Guarantee

Directors provide personal undertaking for business obligations, adding an additional layer of security.

CONTRACTUAL TERMS

Key Terms & Conditions

Clear and transparent terms ensuring mutual understanding and compliance

Key Contractual Terms

Clause 3.1 - 4.5
REF: OE-TER-2024

90-Day Grace Period

To ensure business continuity, Omkar Enterprises maintains a 90-day written notice period for principal withdrawal requests. Purpose: Business stability & capital rotation.

Monthly Returns

2% fixed monthly finance charges paid via NEFT/IMPS on agreed dates. Performance bonuses are discretionary and based on business performance.

Minimum Tenure

8-month minimum lock-in period from the date of agreement execution for all partnership tiers. This ensures stable capital deployment.

Early Withdrawal Terms

Premature withdrawal results in recalculation at 1.5% monthly rate for the actual period, with excess adjusted against principal. This discourages premature exits.

REGULATORY COMPLIANCE

Legal Compliance

Operating within the framework of applicable Indian laws and regulations

Regulatory Compliance

Annexure A
REF: OE-COMP-2024
EXEMPT

SEBI Regulations

Not a collective investment scheme (CIS) as defined under SEBI (CIS) Regulations, 1999. This is a private bilateral agreement.

EXEMPT

RBI Guidelines

Not an NBFC (Non-Banking Financial Company) and does not accept public deposits. All transactions are business-to-business.

COMPLIANT

Indian Contract Act, 1872

All agreements are executed under and governed by the Indian Contract Act, 1872. All essential elements of a valid contract are present.

EXEMPT

BUDS Act 2019

Exempt as per Section 2(4) - "amount received in the course of, or for the purpose of, business and bearing a genuine connection to such business."

DISPUTE RESOLUTION

Conflict Resolution

Structured process for amicable and efficient dispute resolution

Dispute Resolution

Clause 8.1 - 8.7
REF: OE-DR-2024
1

Written Notice

Any dispute must be raised via written notice with 30 days for resolution attempt. Clear documentation of the issue is required.

2

Mediation

If unresolved, parties agree to mediation by a mutually agreed third party. This ensures neutral intervention before legal proceedings.

3

Arbitration

Final binding arbitration in Pune/Thane jurisdiction under Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996. This provides legal enforceability.

Important Legal Notes:

Civil Nature Only

All disputes are civil in nature only. Criminal proceedings require separate civil court determination of fraud.

Limited Liability

Liability is limited to company assets only. Personal assets of directors are protected under corporate veil.
