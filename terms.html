Terms of Partnership

1. Acceptance of Terms

By entering into an investment partnership with Omkar Enterprises, you acknowledge that you have read, understood, and agree to be bound by these Terms and Conditions. These terms constitute a legally binding agreement between you (the "Investor/Partner") and Omkar Enterprises ("the Firm").

2. Investment Partnership Structure

Omkar Enterprises operates as a registered partnership firm facilitating private lending arrangements between investors and businesses. The partnership involves:

  • Investment of capital by the partner
  • Monthly returns at agreed rates (typically 2% per month)
  • PDC (Post Dated Cheque) security for principal amount
  • Notarized partnership agreement
  • Defined tenure with renewal options

3. Returns and Payouts

3.1 Monthly Returns: Partners receive monthly returns on their investment principal. The return percentage is specified in the individual partnership agreement.

3.2 Payout Schedule: Monthly returns are typically paid on the 1st of each month via bank transfer. Exact dates may vary based on banking holidays.

3.3 Return Calculation: Returns are calculated on the principal investment amount. The rate remains fixed for the agreement tenure unless mutually revised.

4. Security Mechanism

4.1 PDC Security: As security for the principal amount, partners provide Post Dated Cheques covering their investment. These are stored securely and returned uncashed upon successful completion of partnership.

4.2 PDC Usage: PDCs may only be deposited in case of default in monthly returns or principal repayment at partnership maturity.

4.3 Return of PDCs: Upon successful completion of partnership term and receipt of principal, all PDCs are returned to the partner.

5. Partnership Tenure and Renewal

5.1 Minimum Tenure: Each partnership has a minimum tenure as specified in the agreement (typically 3, 6, or 12 months).

5.2 Automatic Renewal: Unless notice is given 30 days prior to maturity, partnerships automatically renew for the same tenure.

5.3 Early Termination: Early termination requests are considered on a case-by-case basis and may involve administrative charges.

6. Risk Disclosure

6.1 Market Risks: All investments carry market risks. Past performance is not indicative of future results.

6.2 No Guarantee: While we strive for consistent returns, specific return percentages are projected, not guaranteed.

6.3 Due Diligence: Partners should conduct their own due diligence and consult financial advisors before investing.

6.4 Principal Risk: While PDCs provide security, there is inherent risk in any investment. Partners should only invest surplus funds.

7. Documentation and Verification

7.1 KYC Requirements: Partners must provide valid KYC documents including PAN Card, Aadhar Card, and address proof.

7.2 Agreement Execution: All partnerships require execution of notarized partnership agreements.

7.3 Bank Details: Valid bank account details must be provided for return transfers.

7.4 Updates: Partners must inform us of any changes in contact details or bank information.

8. Confidentiality

Both parties agree to maintain confidentiality of partnership terms, financial arrangements, and proprietary information shared during the partnership.

9. Dispute Resolution

9.1 Amicable Settlement: Parties agree to attempt amicable settlement of disputes before legal proceedings.

9.2 Jurisdiction: Any legal proceedings shall be subject to the jurisdiction of courts in Ulhasnagar, Maharashtra.

9.3 Arbitration: Disputes may be referred to arbitration as per Indian Arbitration and Conciliation Act.

10. Amendments

Omkar Enterprises reserves the right to modify these terms with 30 days notice to partners. Continued partnership constitutes acceptance of modified terms.

11. Force Majeure

Neither party shall be liable for delays or failures in performance resulting from acts beyond reasonable control including natural disasters, government actions, or pandemics.

12. Governing Law

These terms shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of India, specifically the Indian Contract Act 1872 and Partnership Act 1932.

Important Note

These are general terms. Specific partnership agreements may contain additional terms. Please read your individual partnership agreement carefully. These terms do not constitute financial advice. Consult a financial advisor before making investment decisions.
